Current Reality

Challenges Facing Kabiro Ward

Every challenge is an opportunity to serve. Maxwell Changilwa has seen these problems firsthand — and has a plan to fix them.

🛣️

Poor Roads & Infrastructure

Many roads in Kabiro remain unmaintained, making commuting difficult and slowing economic activity for residents and businesses.

💼

Youth Unemployment

Thousands of Kabiro youth remain jobless, lacking platforms, skills training, and connections to real economic opportunities.

🔒

Insecurity

Parts of the ward face persistent insecurity that undermines peace, discourages investment, and traumatizes families.

💧

Lack of Clean Water

Many households lack consistent access to clean, affordable water — a basic human right that remains out of reach.

🚽

Poor Sanitation

Inadequate sanitation infrastructure exposes residents to health risks and affects the dignity of daily life in the ward.

🏗️

Neglected Mjengo Workers

Construction workers face exploitation, unsafe conditions, and no safety nets — despite powering Nairobi's growth.

🏥

Poor Healthcare Access

Residents struggle to access quality and affordable healthcare, with limited county health facilities in proximity.

🏪

Weak Business Support

Small traders and hawkers lack protection, space, and market access to grow their businesses sustainably.

⚽

Lack of Sports Opportunities

Youth talent is wasted without organized sports programs, playgrounds, and pathways to professional development.

Our Vision

Building a Better Kabiro Together

Hon. Maxwell Changilwa's vision is rooted in the everyday struggles of Kabiro residents. Having grown up among these communities, he understands that real change comes from within — driven by servant leadership, transparency, and unwavering commitment.

His agenda is clear: roads, jobs, healthcare, clean water, and dignity for every worker, woman, youth, and family in Kabiro Ward.
