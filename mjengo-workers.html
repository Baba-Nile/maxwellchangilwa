Every Worker Deserves Respect & Fair Pay

The construction workers of Nairobi build the skylines we admire — yet they remain among the most exploited, unprotected, and forgotten workers in Kenya. Maxwell Changilwa says: Not on our watch.

The Reality

What Mjengo Workers Face Every Day

Kabiro's construction workers power Nairobi's growth — but they go home with broken bodies, stolen wages, and no safety nets.

💰
Wage Theft & Exploitation

Contractors routinely withhold or reduce agreed pay. Workers have no written contracts, making legal recourse nearly impossible.

⛑️
No Safety Equipment

Most mjengo sites lack helmets, harnesses, and first aid. Falls and injuries are common — and workers foot the medical bills themselves.

🏥
No Healthcare Access

The majority of construction workers are uninsured — no NHIF, no NSSF. A single accident can plunge an entire family into poverty.

📄
No Formal Contracts

Workers are hired verbally, worked casually, and dismissed without notice or compensation — leaving them legally unprotected.

🕐
Excessive Working Hours

12–16 hour shifts are common with no overtime pay. Workers endure these conditions fearing dismissal if they raise concerns.

🏚️
Housing Insecurity

After working on luxury developments, many mjengo workers return to cramped, substandard housing with no access to basic amenities.

Maxwell's Plan

Fighting For Worker Justice

Maxwell Changilwa has spent years advocating for Kabiro's construction workers. As MCA, he will use every available legal and civic channel to protect and uplift them.

✅Fair Wage Advocacy: Push for county-level minimum wage enforcement for all construction workers in Nairobi
✅Contract Enforcement: Lobby for mandatory written contracts for all casual and permanent mjengo workers
✅Safety Standards: Advocate for building inspection requirements that include worker safety compliance
✅NHIF/NSSF Enrollment: Organize mass enrollment drives to register workers into social protection systems
✅Worker SACCO: Facilitate a Kabiro Mjengo Workers SACCO for savings, loans, and collective bargaining power
✅Legal Clinics: Organize free legal aid clinics for workers to understand and assert their rights
✅Housing Advocacy: Champion affordable housing projects that prioritize ward-level construction workers

🔨 Mjengo SACCO Vision
