Biography

A Son of Kabiro

Hon. Maxwell Changilwa was born and raised in the vibrant community of Kabiro Ward — a place he calls home, his teacher, and his greatest motivation. From a young age, he witnessed firsthand the struggles of ordinary residents: flooded roads, jobless youth, exploited construction workers, and mothers walking miles for clean water.

Rather than leave these challenges behind, Maxwell chose to stay, organize, and fight for his community. He spent over a decade building grassroots networks, mobilizing youth, defending workers, and advocating for women's economic empowerment.

Today, he stands as the voice of the voiceless — the hustler, the mama mboga, the mjengo worker, and the young graduate waiting for their first break.

Leadership Journey

From Streets to Service

Maxwell's path to leadership was forged through years of grassroots work, community organizing, and a relentless belief that Kabiro can be better.

2010 — 2013
Community Youth Organizer

Founded and led the Kabiro Youth Forum, mobilizing hundreds of young people into civic engagement and community clean-up programs.

2014 — 2016
Mjengo Workers Advocate

Partnered with labor organizations to advocate for construction workers' rights, organizing forums on fair wages and workplace safety in Nairobi's informal construction sector.

2017 — 2019
Women Biashara Support

Co-founded a women's microenterprise support group connecting over 200 female traders to affordable credit, business training, and market access.

2020 — 2022
Public Participation Leader

Led public participation forums across Kabiro's sub-locations on county budget allocation, ensuring community voices reached decision-making tables.

2023 — Present
MCA Campaign Launch

Officially announced candidacy for Kabiro Ward MCA seat in the 2027 general elections, with a comprehensive grassroots manifesto born from years of community engagement.

Achievements

— Maxwell Changilwa
